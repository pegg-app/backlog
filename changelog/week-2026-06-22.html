Week of June 22, 2026

Highlights

  • Pricing now uses real sold listings, not guesswork
  • Suggested prices are faster and more accurate than before
  • Some features temporarily hidden to keep the core experience clean

The biggest change this week is a smarter pricing engine — when you go to list an item, Pegg now looks at what similar pieces have actually sold for recently, rather than estimating based on original retail prices. That means the suggested price you see is grounded in real market data, which should help your items sell faster and at fairer prices. The team also did a lot of behind-the-scenes work to stress-test the pricing tool across large batches of items, catching and fixing several accuracy issues before they could reach sellers. A few secondary features were quietly switched off for now, so the app stays focused on the core experience of listing and selling.
